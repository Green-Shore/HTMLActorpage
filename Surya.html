Surya

Surya (/ˈsuːriə/ SOO-ree-ə;[9] Sanskrit: सूर्य, IAST: Sūrya) is the Sun[10] as well as the solar deity in Hinduism.[10] He is traditionally one of the major five deities in the Smarta tradition, all of whom are considered as equivalent deities in the Panchayatana puja and a means to realise Brahman.[11] Other names of Surya in ancient Indian literature include Āditya, Arka, Bhānu, Savitṛ, Pūṣan, Ravi, Mārtāṇḍa, Mitra, Bhāskara, Prabhākara, Kathiravan, and Vivasvat.[12][13][14]
